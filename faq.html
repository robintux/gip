Question Answer
I am enrolled for a combined program of engineering with computer science (CS), I heard that there are basic courses for CS and for engineering-students which one is more appropriate for me? For students from combined-programs we recommend the CS course: the course's level is higher, and there are interesting guest talks given by faculty members.
Is this course obligatory for incoming Computer Science students? No. This course is designated for incoming students with little or no prior experience in programming. It is not obligatory, but recommended for such students.
I have no prior knowledge in programming, can I succeed without taking this course? Many people in the past have succeeded in the first year courses with no past experience in computer science or programming without an introduction course.
What should I do in case I am unable to attend the first (or any other) session? Go over the session's slides and program the examples that will be online from the session's day.
Are we going to be graded on this course? Will it give us academical credit? No. This course does not have a grade and is not "worth" academic points.
How much time should I free up for the course, beside coming to the session? Hearing the official session is not enough! This course is quite intensive for students without prior knowledge - in three weeks you will learn a lot of material. Thus, in order to fully understand you will have to use the time between the sessions to program and exercise what was taught. Practical exercises will be published after every session, it is not obligatory to complete them, but it is highly recommended.
